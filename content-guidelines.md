## Content Categories

### Bridge Lending 101 (Borrower)
Educational content on bridge loan use cases, deal economics, and how short-term financing creates value. Focus on WHEN and WHY to use a bridge loan, not rate shopping or rate comparison. Our rate is 12% interest-only, and that is not the story. The story is: speed kills in competitive markets, bridge loans let you capture deals conventional lenders cannot touch, and the total project economics (not the loan rate) determine whether a deal works.
Target keywords: "bridge loan," "what is a bridge loan," "bridge loan requirements," "when to use a bridge loan," "bridge loan for real estate investors"

### Deal Playbooks (Borrower)
Tactical guides for specific deal types: fix-and-flip, ground-up construction, multifamily value-add, commercial repositioning, mobile home park acquisitions, manufactured housing community investments.
Target keywords: "fix and flip financing," "construction loan process," "mobile home park financing," "MHC investment strategy"

### Market Intelligence (Borrower)
Data-driven market analysis: rate environment, regional opportunities, construction costs, sector-specific trends.
Target keywords: "bridge loan rates 2026," "real estate market trends," "best markets for flipping"

### Borrower Success Stories (Borrower)
Case studies (anonymized or with permission) showing deals financed through Requity Lending.
Structure: problem, financing solution, outcome, timeline.

### Investment Insights (Investor)
Analytical content on private real estate returns, portfolio construction, risk-adjusted performance, fund structures.
Target keywords: "private real estate fund," "bridge lending returns," "real estate debt fund"

### Behind the Portfolio (Investor)
Transparency content: deal selection, underwriting discipline, asset management, portfolio performance.
Target keywords: "real estate underwriting," "private lending investment," "fund transparency"

### SEO Opportunistic (Both)
Comparison articles, trend pieces, keyword-gap content chasing high-volume searches.
Examples: "DSCR Loans Explained," "Best Markets for Real Estate Investing in 2026"

## Writing Standards

### Must Follow
- No fluff intros. First sentence delivers value or states the thesis.
- No em dashes. Use commas, periods, or semicolons.
- Specific numbers over vague claims. "10 business days" not "fast closing."
- Every post ends with a clear CTA relevant to the audience track.
- No first-person plural ("we believe") in Requity Group-branded posts. Use third person or direct address.
- Investor content: institutional tone. No exclamation points, no hype.
- Borrower content: direct, practical tone. Speak to experienced operators.
- Meta descriptions: 150-160 characters, include primary keyword, end with action verb.
- Titles: Under 60 characters for full Google display. Include primary keyword.
- Body content is stored as HTML. Use semantic HTML: h2, h3, p, ul, ol, blockquote, strong, a.
- Do not use h1 tags in body content (the page title serves as h1).

### Never Do
- Do not write about how to get better bridge loan rates, rate shopping, rate negotiation, or rate comparison between lenders. Our rate is 12% interest-only, period. The content should frame bridge loans through the lens of deal economics, speed, and flexibility, not rate optimization.
- Do not position Requity Lending as a "low-rate" or "competitive rate" lender. We compete on speed, certainty, and structure.
- Do not imply bridge loan rates vary at Requity or that borrowers can negotiate terms. Terms are standardized.
- Do not use emojis
- Do not use cliches ("in today's fast-paced world," "at the end of the day")
- Do not use AI-sounding phrases ("it's important to note," "in conclusion," "let's dive in")
- Do not reference AI authorship or generation
- Do not make specific performance promises or guarantees (compliance)
- Do not use the word "lucrative"
- Do not use superlatives without data to back them up

## Database Schema Reference

Posts are inserted into the `site_insights` table in Supabase (project: edhlkknvlczhbowasjna).

Required fields for each post:
```
title: string (under 60 chars)
slug: string (kebab-case, unique)
published_date: date (the scheduled publish date)
excerpt: string (1-2 sentences, under 200 chars)
body_content: string (HTML)
tags: string[] (e.g., ["Bridge Loans", "Mobile Home Parks", "Lending"])
author: string (default "Requity Group")
reading_time_minutes: integer (estimate based on word count / 200)
meta_description: string (150-160 chars, includes primary keyword)
category: string (one of: bridge-lending-101, deal-playbooks, market-intelligence, borrower-stories, investment-insights, behind-the-portfolio, seo-opportunistic)
audience: string (one of: borrower, investor, both)
status: "draft"
is_published: false
sort_order: 0
```

The review process:
1. Task generates posts with status="draft", is_published=false
2. Human reviewer sets status="approved"
3. Auto-publish function sets status="published", is_published=true on the scheduled_publish_date
